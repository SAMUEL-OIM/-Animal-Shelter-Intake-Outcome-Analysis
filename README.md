## â“ Business Questions & Insights

### 1. How do intake and outcome volumes change over time?

* Intake volume shows clear **seasonal patterns**, peaking between **May and July**.
* Intake is highest mid-week, with **Wednesdays recording the most admissions**.
* Intake declines toward the end of the year (Novemberâ€“February).

**Insight:** Shelters experience predictable seasonal and weekly pressure points.
**Implication:** Staffing, kennel capacity, and medical resources should be scaled during peak periods.

---

### 2. How has the number of pet adoptions changed over time?

* While adoption-specific fields were limited, **live outcomes** were used as a proxy.
* Live outcomes remain consistently higher than non-live outcomes across years.

**Insight:** Adoption and live-release performance is generally stable but strained during high-intake periods.

---

### 3. What is the shelterâ€™s live-release rate?

* **Live Outcomes:** 40,479 (~77%)
* **Non-Live Outcomes:** 11,864 (~23%)

**Insight:** Over three-quarters of animals leave the shelter alive, indicating effective baseline operations with room for improvement.

---

### 4. Which types of pets are adopted most often?

* **Cats and Dogs account for over 80% of total intake and live outcomes.**
* Cats slightly exceed dogs in volume but remain longer in shelters.

**Insight:** High intake does not always translate to fast placement.

---

### 5. Which animals have higher or lower live-release rates?

* Animals admitted in **normal condition** have the highest live-release rates.
* **Stray and wildlife intakes** account for a large share of non-live outcomes.

**Insight:** Intake condition and intake type strongly influence survival outcomes.

---

### 6. Do intake types or conditions lead to different outcomes?

* Normal-condition intakes dominate live outcomes.
* Wildlife and stray animals show higher mortality risk.

**Implication:** Early medical screening and rapid intervention for strays could improve outcomes.

---

### 7. What is the average length of stay?

* **Average Length of Stay:** 19 days
* **Cats** account for the longest cumulative shelter stays.
* Wildlife and livestock stays are comparatively short.

---

### 8. Which animals stay the longest in the shelter?

* Cats remain in shelters the longest.
* Characteristics include high intake volume rather than severe medical issues.

**Insight:** Adoption bottlenecks, not health limitations, drive long stays.

---

### 9. Are there seasonal or day-of-week patterns that affect operations?

* Intake spikes mid-week and during warmer months.
* Mondays consistently record the lowest intake.

**Implication:** Resource allocation should reflect predictable intake cycles.

---

### 10. Which intake sources contribute the largest volumes?

* **Stray intakes** represent the largest intake source.
* Wildlife is the second-largest contributor.

**Insight:** Community outreach and prevention programs should focus on stray populations.

---

### 11. Are there repeat intakes?

* Repeat intake analysis was not conducted due to limited re-entry identifiers.
* This represents an opportunity for improved data tracking.

---

### 12. What actions could improve outcomes?

* Expand spay/neuter and stray prevention programs
* Increase staffing and kennel capacity during peak seasons
* Launch targeted cat adoption campaigns
* Improve intake condition assessment and data completeness
